# rei (怜)

You have intentions that matter. Commitments to who you want to become, what you want to build, and how you want to spend your time. But between the intention and the outcome, there's a gap: life is noisy, context is lost, and your best thinking slips away.

**Rei bridges that gap.** It's a system for intentional living where AI agents work alongside you: coaching when you're stuck, researching what you need, executing work you delegate, and running autonomously on schedules you define. The agents don't replace your judgment; they *augment* it.

The name comes from Japanese kanji (怜) meaning "perceptive, wise, emotionally aware," emphasizing clear perception of reality rather than brute control.

## A New Kind of Partnership

Most productivity tools treat you as a task-completion machine. Rei starts from a different premise: **you are a person with intentions**, and the right system helps you see clearly, act deliberately, and get meaningful support when you need it.

## What This Looks Like in Practice

**Morning.** You open your terminal. `rei today` shows your active intentions, what agents worked on overnight, pending reviews from delegated work, and habits due today. An agent nudge suggests focusing on a stalled intention.

**Working.** You delegate a research task to an agent: `rei intention delegate <id> --scope research`. The agent investigates, records findings as notes, and submits a review for your approval. Meanwhile, you're focused on creative work, recording actions, resolving blockers, making progress on what matters most.

**Evening.** `rei agent reflect today` helps you interpret the day's work. The agent asks questions that surface patterns you wouldn't have noticed. Your reflection becomes part of the permanent record.

**Background.** Scheduled agents run on their own: morning briefings, stalled-intention checks, event-triggered responses when blockers are declared. They work while you sleep, but every action is visible and traceable.

## The Deliberate Foundation

Agents amplify whatever system they're built on. Rei's foundation is *intentionality*: nine composable primitives designed for meaning, not throughput.

| | Primitive | What it captures |
|---|-----------|-----------------|
| 🎯 | **Intention** | A commitment to change in the world |
| ⚡ | **Action** | The concrete work you do |
| 📊 | **Outcome** | Observable evidence something changed |
| 🚧 | **Blocker** | What prevents the next meaningful step |
| 🔗 | **Dependency** | Another intention that constrains this one |
| 🔄 | **Habit** | A recurring practice with lifecycle management |
| 💭 | **Reflection** | Deliberate interpretation of past work |
| 🔍 | **Focus** | A category of activity for daily time allocation |
| 📅 | **Cycle** | A bounded time period for tracking focus |

Every event, human and agent alike, is appended to an immutable log. State is always derived, never mutated. This means complete history, no data loss, and the ability to understand exactly what happened and why.

On top of the primitives, **custom properties** let you attach typed metadata (paths, URLs, quantities, state machines) to any entity. These become shared context between you and your agents. A `project_path` tells the agent where your code lives. A `workflow` state machine with enforced transitions, WIP limits, and checklist-gated gates lets you manage complex work with full discipline. Properties make implicit knowledge explicit and queryable, for you and your agents alike.

## Five Principles

1. **Intention-centered.** Organize work by meaning, not checklists.
2. **Agent-augmented.** AI agents coach, assist, execute, and automate within boundaries you control.
3. **Event-sourced truth.** Every change is an immutable fact; state is always derived.
4. **Evidence-based review.** Outcomes and reflections beat memory.
5. **Local-first & transparent.** Your files, your data, nothing hidden.
