Reflections are deliberate interpretations of past work. Unlike actions (which record what you did), reflections capture what you *learned* and what it *means*. They're one of the most powerful points of human-agent collaboration. Agents can prompt deeper thinking by surfacing patterns across your intentions, habits, and actions that you might not notice on your own.

## Why Reflect?

Memory is unreliable. What felt important yesterday fades. Reflection:

- Captures insights while they're fresh
- Builds self-awareness over time
- Creates a record you can reference later
- Transforms raw experience into wisdom
- Gives agents richer context for future coaching and delegation

## Types of Reflections

### Daily Reflection

Reflect on today's work:

```bash
rei reflect today
```

This opens your editor to capture:
- What you accomplished
- What surprised you
- What you learned
- What you'll do differently

### Weekly Reflection

Reflect on the past week:

```bash
rei reflect week
```

Weekly reflection is broader:
- Patterns you noticed
- Progress toward intentions
- What worked and what didn't
- Adjustments for next week

### Intention Reflection

Reflect on a specific intention:

```bash
rei reflect intention <id>
```

Useful when:
- Completing an intention
- Hitting a milestone
- Feeling stuck
- Making a decision

## Reflection Workflow

1. **Open**: Create a new reflection
2. **Write**: Capture your thoughts in your editor
3. **Complete**: Mark the reflection as done
4. **Review**: Reference past reflections when needed

```bash
# See pending reflections
rei reflect pending

# See reflection history
rei reflect history
```

## AI-Assisted Reflection

This is where the agent partnership shines. Instead of staring at a blank page, let an agent prompt your thinking:

```bash
rei agent reflect today
rei agent reflect week
```

The agent reviews your recent actions, completed habits, open blockers, and delegation results. It asks questions that surface patterns:

> *You completed your writing habit 4 of 5 days this week, but skipped Wednesday. You also declared a blocker on your "Become a better writer" intention that day. Is there a connection? What would have made Wednesday different?*

For deeper exploration, use interactive mode:

```bash
rei agent reflect today -i
```

This starts a multi-turn conversation where the agent can dig deeper, help you articulate insights, and even record reflection notes on your behalf.

The agent's memory means reflections compound across sessions. Patterns noticed in week 1 inform questions in week 4.

## Building the Habit

Reflection works best as a regular practice:

- **Daily**: 5 minutes at end of day
- **Weekly**: 15-30 minutes on Sunday
- **Milestone**: whenever you complete something significant

You can also schedule agent-assisted reflection automatically:

```bash
rei agent-schedule add --trigger daily-at:21:00 --scope workspace \
  --guidance "Help me reflect on today's work"
```

The insights compound over time. After a month, you'll have a valuable record of your growth, and an agent that understands your patterns deeply enough to ask better questions.
